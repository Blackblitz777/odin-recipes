Mirchi Bajji (Chilli Fritters)

Description

Mirchi Bajji is a popular, spicy, deep-fried Indian street food made from large green chilies (like banana or jalapeno peppers) stuffed with a savory filling, coated in a spiced gram flour (besan) batter, and fried until crispy and golden brown. It's a popular snack, especially during monsoon season, often served hot with chutneys or sauces.

Ingredients

To make traditional South Indian mirchi bajji (spicy batter-fried green chillies), you need to prepare a spiced chickpea batter for the fritters.

  • Chillies
  • Flour( 1 cup besan (gram flour/chickpea flour) and 2 tablespoons rice flour (for extra crunch))
  • Spices(Carom seeds(Ajwain), Red chilli powder,Asafoetida(hing))
  • Leavening(Baking soda)
  • Stuffing(Tamarind pulp, Cumin powder)
  • Salt

Steps

  1. Preparation and Stuff Chillies Wash and thoroughly dry the green chillies. Make a single vertical slit from top to bottom on one side of each chilli. Use the back of a small spoon to gently scrape out the seeds and white ribs inside to reduce the spicy heat. Mix the tamarind pulp, cumin powder, and salt together. Smear a small amount of this tangy paste inside each slit chilli
  2. Whisk the Batter In a deep mixing bowl, combine the besan, rice flour, carom seeds, chilli powder, asafoetida, baking soda, and a pinch of salt. Gradually whisk in water a few tablespoons at a time until you achieve a thick, smooth, lump-free batter. It should coat the back of a spoon thickly, similar to pancake batter.
  3. Heat the Oil Heat frying oil in a deep pan or kadai over medium-high heat. Test the temperature by dropping a small speck of batter into the oil; it should rise to the surface immediately without browning too quickly.
  4. Dip and Deep Fry Hold a stuffed chilli by its stem. Dip it completely into the thick batter, turning it over to ensure the entire chilli is coated uniformly. Carefully slide the coated chilli into the hot oil. Repeat with 2 or 3 more chillies, being careful not to overcrowd the frying pan.
  5. Drain and Serve Fry the bajjis for 3 to 4 minutes, turning them occasionally. Remove them using a slotted spoon once they turn an even golden-brown and crispy color. Drain the excess oil on paper towels. Serve hot alongside your fresh crispy dosas
